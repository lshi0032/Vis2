What can be done to prevent the spread of HIV?


HIV is most commonly transmitted through unprotected sex and drug injection equipment.
It is therefore very important for countries that have high cases of HIV to encourage the use
of condoms during sex to prevent the transmission of HIV.
Condoms are 90% to 95% effective at preventing infection.

Infections vs Condom use

It would make sense for HIV infections to decrease as condom use becomes more frequent but this data does not suggest this.
This may be due to the fact that countries with high condom use have high rates of sex and as a result, those that do not practise
safe sex are spreading it more quickly.

How does HIV impact age groups?

It appears people aged 15-49 suffered the most deaths from HIV from 1990-2019.
Within this group, males aged 20-24 were the most frequenct users of condoms.
